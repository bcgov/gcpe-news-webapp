How to get vaccinated for COVID-19

Getting vaccinated is easy and safe. Spread the word and help your friends and family complete their registration, book an appointment and get the vaccine.

The information on this page is available in 12 languages. 

Last updated: April 23, 2021 

Step 1: Register

Registration is open to anyone born in 2003 or earlier (18+).

Register now

When you register, you will receive a confirmation number. Do not lose the number. You'll need it later to book your vaccination appointment.

I lost my confirmation number

I made a mistake in my registration

Step 2: Book an appointment

Don't expect to book an appointment the same day you register. 

You will receive a text, email or phone call when you're eligible to book an appointment.

We're currently sending booking invitations to:

  • People born in 1961 or earlier (60+)
  • Indigenous people born in 2003 or earlier (18+)
  • Clinically extremely vulnerable people

Step 3: Get the vaccine

Visit the vaccine clinic to get your vaccine dose.

People who get their first vaccine dose will be notified by email, text or phone call when they are eligible to book an appointment for their second dose.

I already received the AstraZeneca vaccine

I need to get dose 2

How to register

You can register yourself or someone else, like a parent or grandparent. Everyone 18 and older in B.C. is eligible to get vaccinated, even if you don’t have a Personal Health Number or other documentation.

We protect all information we collect and public health will never share your information with any other agencies or parts of government. We will never ask you for your SIN, driver's licence number or banking and credit card details. 

Fastest option: Register online with a Personal Health Number

Register online It takes 2 minutes

To register online, you must provide:

  • First and last name
  • Date of birth 
  • Postal code
  • Personal Health Number
  • An email address that gets checked regularly or a phone number that can receive text messages

Click card to enlarge

Find your Personal Health Number on the back of your B.C. driver's licence, BC Services Card or CareCard. 

Registration options if you don't have a Personal Health Number

If you don't have a Personal Health Number, you need to register by phone or at a Service BC office. They will create a Personal Health Number for you.

Register by phone

Call: 1-833-838-2323 | Translators are available

Seven days a week, 7 am to 7 pm (PDT)

Telephone for the Deaf: Dial 711

Register at a Service BC office

You can register in-person at all Service BC offices. 

Office hours vary by location. Check before you go. 

  • Find a Service BC office close to me

What to expect at the vaccine clinic

We recommend you review information on COVID-19 vaccine safety from HealthlinkBC before your clinic visit. 

Arrive prepared

Get ready for your appointment:

  • Wear a short-sleeved shirt and a mask
  • Arrive a few minutes before your scheduled appointment time

You can bring one person with you for support.

All clinics are wheelchair accessible. You will be provided a mask if you need one. 

During the appointment

At the clinic you will:

  • Complete a check-in process
  • Get either the Pfizer or Moderna vaccine dose. A choice will not be offered
  • Wait in an observation area for about 15 minutes

You can expect to be at the clinic for 30 to 60 minutes in total.

I lost my registration confirmation number

Call 1-833-838-2323. The call centre team can look-up your number.

Do not register again.

 

I made a mistake in my registration

Call 1-833-838-2323. The call centre team can correct the information for you.

Do not register again.

 

I already received the AstraZeneca vaccine

If you haven't received an invitation to book your first dose appointment

The booking system will automatically update after you receive your first dose of the AstraZeneca vaccine at a pharmacy.

Starting May 1, register for your second dose. You will then get a notification when you are eligible to book your second dose. 

If you have received an invitation to book your first dose appointment

Do not book another appointment. If you book an appointment, it will be cancelled because you have already received your first dose.

You will get a notification when you are eligible to book your second dose. 

If you already had a first dose appointment booked, you need to cancel it

Option 1: Cancel online

Click on the appointment confirmation link you received by email or text message after booking the appointment.

Enter the confirmation number and your Personal Health Number.

Select the option to cancel the appointment.

You will get a cancellation notice with another confirmation code. Do not book another appointment.

Option 2: Cancel by phone

Call 1-833-838-2323. The call centre team can cancel the appointment for you.

Wait to receive an invitation to book your second dose

If you have registered, you will get an email, text message or phone call when it's time to book your second dose.

 

I need to get dose 2

I want to schedule my dose 2 appointment

If you have registered, you will get an email, text message or phone call when it's time to book your second dose.

I received dose 1 before the registration system was available and I need to get dose 2

Starting May 1, register for your second dose. You will then get a notification when you are eligible to book your second dose.
